Don't this Road Look Rough and Rocky

Photo credit to Benoit Deschasaux

This is a song of somewhat uncertain origins, although the most popular version that is currently sung today is the one recorded by Flatt and Scruggs in 1954. The song lyrics bear a strong similarity to other, older songs, such as “Can’t You Hear that Night Bird Crying” recorded by the Blue Sky Boys in 1937, and also “Little Bunch of Roses” recorded by Clarence Greene in 1928.

One of the tricky things to remember about this song is that in the first line of the melody, there’s a quick 4 chord amidst the otherwise straight line of 1 chords. The chorus starts also starts on a 4 chord for a couple measures before dropping back to the 1.

Flatt and Scruggs played the song at a somewhat jaunty tempo, which in my opinion detracts a bit from the sadness of the lyrics. I find this beautiful rendition by Aoife O’Donovan and Noam Pikelny to be more to my liking. I’ve tried singing this song at jams at this tempo, and from first-hand experience I have to say that one of the downsides is that I often run out of breath before I get to the end of the line!
