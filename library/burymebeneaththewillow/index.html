Bury Me Beneath the Willow

Photo by Jan Antonin Kolar on Unsplash

This is a song has been around a long time, and also has a variety of titles, include “The Weeping Willow”, “The Willow Tree”, “Under the Willow Tree”. One of the earlier citations of the song with sheet music was published in 1927 in the book “American mountain songs”, compiled by Ethel Park Richardson. The lyrics are in this early publication are very similar to lyrics that are sung today to this song, but the melody and rhythm are substantially difference. Many prominent oldtime/bluegrass/genre-adjacent artists have recorded version of this song, including the Carter family in 1927, Ricky Skaggs and Tony Rice in 1980, and more recently, Michael Daves and Chris Thile in 2011.

The song is fairly straightforward, with no particularly tricky chords or quirks in its chord progression, and the melody for the verse and chorus are the same. It’s usually played at a fairly moderate tempo, and offers a great opportunity for some beautiful harmonies on the chorus.
