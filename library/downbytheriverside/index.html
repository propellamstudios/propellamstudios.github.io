Down by the Riverside

Photo by Dave on Unsplash

This song is an African-American spiritual, also known under the title “Ain’t Gonna Study War No More,” and was first recorded by the Fisk University Jubilee Singers in 1920. It’s been recorded by a variety of singers since then, including Sister Rosetta Tharpe, Mahalia Jackson, Louis Armstrong, Elvis Presley, and Pete Seeger. The song also became popular in the 1960’s as an anti-Vietnam protest song.

The song is not commonly part of the bluegrass repertoire, but it fits fairly well in the tradition of gospel, and there’s certainly a long history of bluegrass borrowing from African-American spiritual songs. Given the repetitive nature of the lyrics, it’s a fairly easy song to improvise to, and for folks to join in harmonizing on the verses as well as the chorus.
