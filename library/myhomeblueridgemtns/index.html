My Home's Across the Blue Ridge Mountains

Photo by Wes Hicks on Unsplash

This is an old song that has been sung with a variety of lyrics and known by a variety of titles, e.g. “I’m Going Back to North Carolina”, “My Home’s Across the Smoky Mountains,” or “My Home’s in Charlotte, North Caroline.” Perhaps the earliest reference to this song was in the Journal of American Folklore in the article “Ballads and Songs of Western North Carolina” by Louise Rand Bascom, where lyrics but no musical score is published. In that article, the lyrics are published under the title “My Own True Love”, and the lyrics are different from the common modern lyrics, except for that characteristic last line - “And I never expect to see you any more.”

Probably the earliest recording was by the Carolina Tar Heels in 1929, but the song was also recorded by the Carter Family in 1938, and then later on by many bluegrass/oldtime/adjacent artists, including the Bluegrass Album Band in 1989.

This is an easy song to lead at jams, since the lyrics are very repetitive and easy to improvise on, and the melody for the verse and chorus is the same. What’s more, this is a two chord song - just 1 and 5! It’s usually sung at a somewhat slow tempo, in keeping with the plaintive lyrics.
